# Transcript Anti-Patterns

## Purpose

Use this file during transcript generation and transcript review to reject common bad narration patterns.

## Anti-Pattern 1: Character Roleplay

Bad:

> I watched the blast shake the whole crowd, and I knew something was wrong.

Reason:

The transcript becomes first-person roleplay. The default recap voice is external narration.

Better:

> The blast shocks the crowd, turning the moment from playful setup into sudden impact.

## Anti-Pattern 2: Speech-Bubble Paraphrase

Bad:

> Toph asks what fireworks sound like for her, then everyone hears a loud boom.

Reason:

This repeats dialogue and surface action but may miss the panel function.

Better:

> Toph's question sets up the gag, and the next panel pays it off with the explosive sound she was describing.

## Anti-Pattern 3: Unsupported Emotion

Bad:

> Everyone realizes they may not survive the night.

Reason:

This invents stakes unless the panels support mortal danger.

Better:

> The sudden boom startles the group and interrupts the calm fireworks moment.

## Anti-Pattern 4: Isolated Panel Thinking

Bad:

> The characters react to a loud explosion.

Reason:

Accurate but incomplete if the prior panel sets up a joke or reveal.

Better:

> The reaction completes the previous setup, turning Toph's question into a physical punchline.

## Anti-Pattern 5: Visual Detail Dump

Bad:

> Two characters in green clothing stand beneath a night sky while fireworks explode, then they open their mouths and raise their hands.

Reason:

This is panel description, not recap narration.

Better:

> The peaceful fireworks scene flips into shock when the punchline lands as a deafening blast.

## Anti-Pattern 6: Fake Certainty

Bad:

> Aang becomes angry because he knows the enemy is nearby.

Reason:

This invents identity, emotion, and motive unless confirmed by artifacts.

Better:

> A character reacts with alarm, though the cause and identity need review.
