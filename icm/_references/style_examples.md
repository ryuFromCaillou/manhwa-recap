# Style Examples

## Purpose

This file gives paired examples for recap narration. These examples are reusable across chapters.

## Example: Setup and Payoff

Input idea:

A character asks what fireworks sound like to her. In the next panel, a huge boom shocks the group.

Bad output:

> I wondered what fireworks sounded like, and then boom, everyone was scared.

Good output:

> Toph sets up the gag by framing fireworks through sound, and the next panel pays it off with a boom that jolts everyone around her.

## Example: Simple Action

Input idea:

A character runs through a hallway while others chase behind.

Bad output:

> He knew he had to escape before his enemies destroyed everything he loved.

Good output:

> The chase continues as he sprints down the hallway with the others close behind.

## Example: Uncertain Identity

Input idea:

A figure in dark clothing appears in shadow. The identity is unclear.

Bad output:

> Zuko appears in the shadows, preparing his next move.

Good output:

> A shadowed figure appears, but the panel does not provide enough evidence to confirm who it is.

## Example: Dialogue Present

Input idea:

A speech bubble says, "We have to leave now."

Bad output:

> They say they have to leave now.

Good output:

> The dialogue pushes the scene into motion, making it clear the group has to leave immediately.

## Example: Reaction Panel

Input idea:

A close-up shows a character shocked by what was just revealed.

Bad output:

> She is shocked.

Good output:

> Her reaction gives the reveal weight, showing that the previous information changes how the group understands the situation.
